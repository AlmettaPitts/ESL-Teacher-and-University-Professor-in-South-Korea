## This Is Not My First Time

I am not arriving in South Korea as a stranger.

I lived and worked there for **4.5 years** — as an EPIK co-teacher at Sinju Middle School in Yangsan-si and as a Visiting Associate Professor at Dankook University and Baekseok University in Cheonan. I know what it means to build relationships slowly and keep them. I know how to navigate a school culture that values collective care, respect, and commitment to students. I left in 2015 not because I wanted to — but because the next chapter called me forward.

Now I am ready to return.

I come back more qualified, more practiced, and more certain than ever that South Korea is where my work belongs. A decade of clinical depth, university teaching, and international consulting has only deepened what I first discovered in those classrooms in Yangsan and Cheonan: that education, when it centers the whole student, changes lives.
